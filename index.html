How The Story Engine Works
What This Is
The Story Engine is a pitch coaching tool that teaches you six of the most powerful storytelling frameworks ever developed — then helps you apply all of them to your own idea, one layer at a time. By the end, you won't just have a pitch. You'll have a complete narrative architecture you can deploy in any room, for any audience, in any amount of time.
The Flow — How It Builds
This isn't six separate exercises. It's one deepening conversation. Every framework is a sharper lens on the same idea — your idea. The logline compresses it. The spine gives it emotional arc. Made to Stick makes it land for strangers. The Golden Circle reveals the belief underneath it. The Powerwall makes inaction feel dangerous. The Strategic Narrative gives your audience a story where they are the hero.

Each output feeds the next. Nothing gets thrown away. By Framework 06, you'll have built something that most CEOs spend entire careers trying to articulate. The narrative clarity that made Steve Jobs the most studied presenter in history. The pitch architecture that helped Elon Musk convince the world that electric cars were inevitable before anyone believed it. The storytelling instincts that separate the founders who actually change industries from the ones who just had good ideas.

Most people who reach that level spend decades figuring it out by trial and error — if they figure it out at all. You're building it now, from first principles, with intention. That's not a small thing. That's where this is going.
How a Session Works
Each framework follows the same pattern. The coach teaches first — real examples, real craft analysis, no padding. Then it asks about your idea and coaches you through building it, one Socratic question at a time. It will push back on weak answers. That's not a bug. When you've got something strong, it delivers the finalized output and the next framework unlocks automatically.
The Six Frameworks
01
Hollywood Logline — Compress your entire idea into one sentence with a protagonist, a goal, an obstacle, and stakes. The test of whether you actually know what your idea is.
02
Pixar Story Spine — Stretch your idea into a full emotional arc. Eight beats that force genuine causality — not just what happens, but what each event makes inevitable.
03
Made to Stick — Diagnose why your idea might not be remembered. Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories. Find the gaps. Fix them.
04
Golden Circle — Find the belief underneath the idea. Why → How → What. People don't act on information. They act on conviction. This is where you find yours.
05
Powerwall Structure — Build the case for action. Name the enemy, declare why now, paint the destination, kill the objections, present the proof, make the ask. World-first, not product-first.
06
Strategic Narrative — Give the audience a story where they are the hero. Name the undeniable shift, show who wins and who loses, paint the Promised Land, hand them the magic gifts, prove the journey works.
The Pitch Room
Unlocks after all six frameworks are complete. This is where everything you built gets deployed. Your advisor — who has watched you build the whole thing from scratch — is now fully in your corner. Reformat your pitch for any audience or time constraint. Get stress-tested by a skeptical investor or a teacher who thinks you're dreaming too big. Get a specific action plan for wherever you're actually taking this. Run a weakness audit. Find the through-line across all six frameworks. This is where your story comes alive.
Saving Your Work
Hit Save for later in the sidebar after each framework. This downloads a JSON file with your full conversation history and all finalized outputs. To pick up where you left off, hit Resume session and load that file. Everything restores — your idea, your outputs, your conversation. Don't skip the save. The work you do in here is worth keeping.
A Few Tips
→
Give rough answers. The coach sharpens them. Waiting until your answer is perfect before typing it is the wrong move — the push-back is the point.
→
One framework at a time. Don't rush to the next one. The depth you put in here compounds in every framework that follows.
→
The Pitch Room is where it gets real. Everything before it is preparation. Come in with a specific room you need to walk into — a teacher, a competition, a real investor — and your advisor will get you ready.
